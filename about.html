About Us

About the Hackathon

Everybody Hacks! is UW’s newest interdisciplinary hackathon, created for students of all majors. We welcome projects ranging between app design, short stories, policy assessments, data visualizations, map atlases, historical reviews, infrastructure blueprints, visual art--you name it!

The hackathon is a fun chance to collaborate with your peers to develop a product from scratch and hack some real world issues. Beat the clock while you explore how your unique skills can be used in collaborative spaces to tackle problems that matter to you.

Our Timeline

The Hackathon is scheduled for the weekend following Week 7 in Spring quarter. In addition to the main event, the Everybody Hacks Planning Committee is organizing a "Countdown to the Hackathon" event series between weeks 2-5 of Spring quarter. This series is designed to include four panel-style events, featuring faculty and professionals from a variety of disciplines, each discussing how they use their particular skills from their field for social good.

For more information about the Hackathon and Countdown event series, visit our Events page or join our “Everybody Hacks! Events” Discord Server.

Origins

Everybody Hacks was founded in 2021 by GeoDat, the Society for Geography & Data Science, which is a research club at UW focused on investigating social issues. Most often, hackathons are designed for projects related to computer science and information technology. Having participated in (and really enjoyed) a few hackathons ourselves, we decided to start our own--one where we could make any kind of projects we wanted, tech or no tech!

We also thought it would also be a great way to foster interdisciplinary connections on campus, and encourage all of us to step outside our majors to learn about each other’s skills and strengths. GeoDat’s goal for this hackathon is to invite all undergraduate students on campus to engage in a hackathon environment and work together on projects of their choosing. It’s a chance to hone our skills and learn about each other, all while working toward social good!

To learn more about GeoDat, visit our LinkTree or email us
